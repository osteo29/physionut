1. ACL Reconstruction (Hamstring Graft) Phase 1 - Immediate Post-op (Week 0-2)
GOALS
- Control pain & swelling
- Achieve full passive knee extension
- Quadriceps activation
- Independent ambulation with crutches
PRECAUTIONS
- No active knee extension 0-90°
- Avoid hamstring loading (graft protection)
- PWB with crutches
Exercise Parameters Clinical Cue / Rationale
Quadriceps Sets (Isometric) 3 x 20 reps, 5-sec hold
Flatten back of knee to bed, feel VMO contract
Ankle Pumps 3 x 30 reps Reduce DVT risk, improve circulation
Straight Leg Raise (SLR) 3 x 15 reps Lock knee before lifting, no lag allowed
Heel Slides (AAROM Flexion) 3 x 15 reps Slide heel toward buttocks, hold 2 sec at end range
Passive Knee Extension (prone hang)
3 x 5 min Let gravity achieve full extension
Patellar Mobilisation 2 x 2 min Medial, lateral, superior glides - pain-free
CRITERIA TO PROGRESS TO NEXT PHASE
- Full passive extension achieved
- SLR without extension lag
- Swelling <1+ on effusion scale
Phase 2 - Early Strengthening (Week 2-6)
GOALS
- 0-120° ROM
- Normalise gait without crutches
- Begin CKC loading
- Neuromuscular re-education
PRECAUTIONS
- Avoid OKC knee extension 0-60°
- No pivoting or cutting
- Monitor effusion with activity
Exercise Parameters Clinical Cue / Rationale
Mini Squats (0-45°) 3 x 15 reps Knees track over 2nd toe, weight through heels
Step-Ups (10 cm box) 3 x 15 reps each leg Lead with affected leg, control descent
Leg Press (60-90°) 3 x 15 reps, light load Bilateral then unilateral, avoid locking out
Terminal Knee Extension (band) 3 x 20 reps Focus on last 20° - VMO activation
Hip Abduction - Side-lying 3 x 20 reps Maintain pelvic stability, control return
Calf Raises (bilateral) 3 x 20 reps Full ROM, slow eccentric phase
Stationary Bike (low resistance) 20 min ROM & cardiovascular - no pain
CRITERIA TO PROGRESS TO NEXT PHASE
- Full ROM 0-120°
- Single-leg stance >=30 sec
- Normal gait pattern without device
Phase 3 - Advanced Strengthening (Week 6-12)
GOALS
- Limb Symmetry Index (LSI) >70%
- Begin jogging
- Develop strength & power
- Proprioception training
PRECAUTIONS
- No change-of-direction sport
- Monitor for effusion post-exercise
- Gradual plyometric loading
Exercise Parameters Clinical Cue / Rationale
Bulgarian Split Squat 4 x 10 reps each Front foot flat, torso upright, 90° knee flexion
Romanian Deadlift 4 x 10 reps Hip hinge pattern, neutral spine, feel hamstring load
Lateral Band Walks 3 x 15 steps each direction
Maintain squat position, band around ankles
Single-Leg Press 4 x 12 reps Progressive loading, track limb symmetry
Box Step-Down 3 x 12 reps each Slow 5-sec eccentric, control valgus collapse
Double-leg Squat Jump 3 x 10 reps Soft landing, absorb force through hips and knees
Forward Hop (non-reactive) 3 x 8 reps Stick landing 3 sec before next hop
Linear Jog Program Start 10 min, progress weekly
Even surface, no cutting - pain-free
CRITERIA TO PROGRESS TO NEXT PHASE
- LSI >=80% quad & hamstring strength
- Hop test symmetry >=80%
- Jog pain-free 30 min
Phase 4 - Return to Sport (Month 4-9)
GOALS
- LSI >=90% all hop tests
- Sport-specific agility
- Psychological readiness
- Full return to unrestricted sport
PRECAUTIONS
- Clear psychological readiness (ACL-RSI score)
- Minimum 9 months post-op for contact sport
- Gradual reintroduction to team training
Exercise Parameters Clinical Cue / Rationale
T-Agility Test Drills 4-6 sets Progressive speed increase over weeks
Reactive Change-of-Direction 3 x 10 reps Controlled, then progress to reactive cues
Plyometric Progression (single-leg)
3 x 8 reps Lateral hops, bounding, depth jumps - master each before progressing
Sport-Specific Drills 3 x 5 min blocks Cutting, deceleration, sport movement patterns
Strength Maintenance (Gym) 3 x week Maintain gains achieved in Phase 3
CRITERIA TO PROGRESS TO NEXT PHASE
- LSI >=90% quad/hamstring/hop
- ACL-RSI >=65
- Clearance from surgeon + physio
